As for the overall style of the website: 


industrial sci-fi + heroic machinery + neon energy restrained by darkness
It is not colorful in a playful way. It is mostly dark, metallic, and atmospheric, with color used as controlled ignition points. The palette works because most of the frame is subdued, and then a few colors punch through:
deep black-blue space tones
gunmetal / steel neutrals
embers and molten orange
warning red
electric cyan / cold blue glow
small traces of violet-magenta light
So the feeling is not “bright futuristic.”
It is more:
cinematic, mechanical, high-contrast, battle-worn, sleek, luminous in selective places
That makes it a very strong fit for a site that starts in a dark hero and then resolves into a cleaner white layout.
What the palette is actually doing
Across the images, the colors fall into 4 functional groups:
1. Base world colors
These build the environment.
Void black — almost true black, but slightly blue/purple
Midnight navy
charcoal gunmetal
smoky steel gray
These should dominate the hero section.
Approximate usable web values:
#06070B
#0C1119
#131A24
#1E2630
#2F3742
These give you that “space hangar / mech bay / deep metallic” feel.
2. Heat colors
These give the images their aggression and drama.
burnt orange
molten amber
rusted copper
deep ember red-orange
Approximate usable web values:
#C96A1B
#E38A2C
#A84A16
#6E2312
These should be your strongest accent family if you want the site to feel powerful and cinematic.
Use them for:
CTA hover states
key icons
selected tabs
small section dividers
glows behind buttons
underline accents
3. Signal colors
These are the “energy core” colors.
electric cyan
cold blue-white
ice blue
small violet/lavender glow
Approximate usable web values:
#79D9FF
#5FB7FF
#B7F1FF
#8D78FF
These should be used more sparingly than the warm tones.
They feel like optics, LEDs, interface light, reflections on metal.
Use them for:
link hover glow
subtle gradients in cards
input focus rings
micro-animations
thin borders on dark sections
icon highlights
4. Armor colors
These are important because the bright colors only work against them.
desaturated steel
cold silver
battle-worn gray
muted slate blue-gray
Approximate usable web values:
#707A86
#8D97A3
#A6AFB8
#5B6675
These are excellent for typography and structure inside the dark hero.
Best synthesis for your site
Since you want:
a dark space hero section that fades to white, cleaner, sleek
the strongest approach is this:
Hero section
Make it feel like a cinematic command deck / space hangar.
Use:
background: deep navy-black gradient
subtle steel-blue haze
very faint orange and cyan bloom in opposite areas
metallic typography or crisp white type with restrained glow
accents: orange first, cyan second
A strong hero background direction would be something like:
top: #05070B
middle: #0C1520
lower transition: #1B2430
then fade into #F7F9FC or #FFFFFF
So the page feels like it is emerging from darkness into clarity.
The key design principle: don’t overuse the bright colors
The images work because the vivid tones are localized.
For your site, that means:
70–80% should be neutrals
10–15% should be cool steel / blue-grays
5–10% should be warm orange/red accents
2–5% should be electric cyan/violet highlights
If you make everything saturated, you lose the cinematic/mechanical tension.

How this should feel in different sections
Dark hero
background: black/navy gradient
headline: white or slightly steel-tinted white
CTA primary: molten orange
CTA secondary: transparent or steel outline with cyan hover
ambient glows: very soft cyan and ember
Transition section
This is where the site should start “cooling down.”
Bring in:
smoky blue-grays
very soft warm separators
less glow, more precision
White / clean sections
This is where many people lose the vibe.
Do not abandon the sci-fi identity entirely.
Keep it alive through:
thin slate borders
orange micro-accents
blue focus states
metallic icon treatment
subtle gradient overlays in cards
Think:
clean editorial tech site with hidden mech-energy underneath
not:
“dark game landing page pasted onto a SaaS site”
Material / texture direction
The images also suggest texture, not just color.
You want surfaces that feel like:
brushed metal
illuminated panels
polished armor edges
slight battle wear
fog, bloom, atmospheric depth
On a website, that translates to:
soft radial glows
slight gradient noise
subtle bevel-like edge lighting
panel divisions
restrained shadows with cool undertones
Avoid:
flat candy gradients
playful neon
overly glassmorphic pastel UI
bright primary reds/blues with no depth
Typography vibe
Even if your layout becomes clean and white later, the palette suggests typography that feels:
bold
engineered
geometric
slightly condensed
sharp-edged rather than rounded
For headings, think:
industrial grotesk
square/geometric sans
slightly extended or condensed techno forms
Body text should stay highly readable and modern, but your display type can carry the “mech / sci-fi” flavor.

a polished futuristic interface emerging out of a war-torn cinematic sci-fi world

the top of the page is dark, atmospheric, metallic, and high-drama
the lower sections become bright, disciplined, and premium
color is not decorative; it behaves like energy, heat, and signal
orange gives power and heat
