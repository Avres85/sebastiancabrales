# Merged Portfolio Style Direction

## Purpose
This document defines the merged visual direction for the portfolio site.

The site structure remains the one already described in the `main_site` folder:

1. hero section with the existing main animation
2. horizontal row of 3 movie GIFs
3. looping figure collection carousel with interactive figure buttons linking to review pages
4. modular technical projects section

The content types, order, motion system, and overall layout logic stay the same.

What changes is the stylistic approach:

- keep the dark industrial sci-fi palette and cinematic energy from the existing `main_site` docs
- merge in the sharp, restrained, editorial, high-fashion line work and typographic severity described in `off-white.md`
- preserve the existing animation, carousel, GIFs, and projects rather than replacing them

## Core Thesis
The portfolio should feel like:

**industrial sci-fi machinery presented with luxury-editorial discipline**

That means:

- the current site structure stays content-driven and modular
- the palette stays dark, metallic, ember-lit, and selectively electric
- the presentation becomes harder, cleaner, sharper, and more self-controlled
- the UI should feel more like a designed visual system than a playful portfolio collage

This is not an Off-White copy.
It is a translation of Off-White's strongest digital behaviors into this portfolio's existing universe.

## What To Borrow From Off-White
The traits to borrow are structural and stylistic, not literal brand imitation.

### 1. Sharp-line graphic discipline
Use straight rules, hard dividers, strong section frames, tight edge alignments, and deliberate borders.

The site should feel cut, measured, and assembled.
Not soft, rounded, or bubbly.

### 2. Restrained confidence
Keep text concise.
Let images, animation, and media do most of the emotional work.
Avoid over-explaining sections with large paragraphs.

### 3. Editorial hierarchy
Use short uppercase labels, strong headlines, controlled spacing, and image-first sequencing.
Each section should read like a designed spread, not like stacked generic cards.

### 4. Repeated graphic codes
Introduce a small number of repeating visual motifs across the site so the whole experience feels authored.

Recommended recurring motifs:

- horizontal rule lines
- framed content blocks
- small section labels
- hard-edged borders
- diagonal slash accents used sparingly
- index-style numbering for sections

### 5. Compression over decoration
The interface should not become busy.
It should become more severe.
The luxury feeling comes from control, not from adding more styling noise.

## What Must Stay From The Existing Main Site Direction
These are preserved:

- dark-to-light cinematic page flow
- existing hero animation as the opening statement
- row of 3 GIFs immediately below
- looping figure showcase with interactive links
- project section as a reusable module system
- current emphasis on industrial sci-fi atmosphere
- current warm/cool contrast of ember orange and electric cyan

## Color System
The palette stays rooted in the previously agreed `main_site` colors.
The Off-White influence should affect shape language and hierarchy, not replace the color world.

### Primary darks
- Void Black: `#06070B`
- Midnight Metal: `#0D121A`
- Deep Space Navy: `#111B27`
- Gunmetal: `#222B35`

### Structural neutrals
- Steel Slate: `#4F5C6B`
- Titanium Gray: `#7B8694`
- Soft Alloy: `#B5BDC8`

### Warm accents
- Molten Orange: `#D97A24`
- Forge Amber: `#F0A13A`
- Burnt Copper: `#A94F1D`
- Warning Red: `#8F1F1A`

### Cool accents
- Energon Blue: `#67C7FF`
- Cold Cyan: `#9BE7FF`
- Ice Glow: `#D9F7FF`
- Plasma Violet: `#8E79FF`

### Light transition tones
- Mist White: `#F7F9FC`
- Clean Panel White: `#FFFFFF`

## Color Usage Rules
To keep the merged look disciplined:

- 70 to 80 percent of the screen should remain dark neutrals or light neutrals depending on section
- warm accents should be the primary energy signal
- cool accents should be secondary and more surgical
- violet should remain a trace color, not a headline color
- bright colors should appear as ignitions, underlines, focus states, micro-glows, and small graphic markers

This keeps the site cinematic and sharp rather than neon-saturated.

## Typography Direction
The typography should merge motorsport-industrial energy with editorial restraint.

### Display / figure headings
Use the previously described stack:

`Eurostile, Microgramma, "Bank Gothic", "Arial Black", sans-serif`

Treatment:

- uppercase
- italic
- bold or black
- slightly extended tracking
- compact line-height

This is especially important for:

- figure names
- section headers
- key hero callouts

### Supporting text
Body text should be cleaner and more neutral than the display type.
The display face provides aggression.
The body face should provide clarity and control.

### Typographic behavior inspired by Off-White
- short uppercase section labels
- minimal descriptive paragraphs
- headlines that read as statements, not as marketing blurbs
- controlled spacing around headings
- high contrast between headline severity and body restraint

## Shape Language
This is the main area where the Off-White-style sharpness should enter the portfolio.

### Use more of
- straight horizontal and vertical rule lines
- rectangular frames
- panel divisions
- thin but assertive borders
- clipped or angled accent details used sparingly
- grid alignment that feels intentional and engineered

### Use less of
- soft blobs
- excessive rounded corners
- floating shadow-heavy cards
- playful gradients
- decorative shapes with no structural role

### Recommended edge treatment
- small radius or no radius
- thin steel borders on dark sections
- stronger black or alloy borders on light sections
- occasional diagonal line or corner-cut used as a repeating motif

## Motion Direction
The motion content stays in place, but the presentation around it becomes more disciplined.

The site already has enough moving parts:

- hero animation
- GIF row
- looping figure carousel

Because of that, the new style system should make containers and transitions feel controlled rather than hyperactive.

Motion rules:

- let the hero remain the dominant kinetic statement
- keep GIF presentation framed and clean
- keep carousel movement smooth and readable
- use restrained fades, line wipes, and subtle panel reveals rather than flashy transforms
- respect reduced motion and do not stack multiple aggressive animations at once

## Section-By-Section Style Merge

## 1. Hero Section
### Layout
Keep the existing main animation as the landing experience.
Do not change the hero's role in the page hierarchy.

### Style treatment
Apply the merged style by adding:

- a hard-edged frame or overlay grid around the hero content
- restrained section labeling
- sharp typographic callouts instead of soft supporting text blocks
- subtle line-based UI chrome that suggests a machine interface or editorial layout system

### Color behavior
- base should be Void Black through Deep Space Navy
- use ember orange as the main CTA or focal accent
- use cyan only for secondary highlights, edge glow, or interface-like micro details

### Desired feeling
The hero should feel like a luxury industrial launch bay:
precise, expensive, atmospheric, and controlled.

## 2. Three-GIF Row
### Layout
Keep the current structure: 3 movie GIFs arranged horizontally.

### Style treatment
Instead of treating them as casual media thumbnails, present them as framed editorial stills:

- equal visual weight
- hard borders
- short uppercase labels
- aligned captions if captions are present
- sharp spacing between panels

### Color behavior
Let the GIFs provide most of the visual variety.
Surrounding UI should stay quiet and structural.

### Desired feeling
This row should read like a triptych or visual insert spread, not a casual gallery strip.

## 3. Figure Carousel
### Layout
Keep the looping horizontal movement and keep the figure items interactive.
Do not replace the carousel logic.

### Style treatment
This section should become the clearest blend of the two source styles.

Each figure item should feel like a branded industrial product panel:

- sharp frame
- bold uppercase figure title in the specified font stack
- small metadata area if needed
- restrained hover emphasis
- clear link affordance

### Figure-specific accent bars
The three-color mini bar described in `main_site/style.txt` should remain.
That bar becomes a strong recurring graphic element.

Rules:

- each figure card includes the 3-color signature strip
- those colors should reflect the figure itself
- the strip should be small, intentional, and repeated consistently
- it should behave like a coded product marker rather than decorative candy

### Desired feeling
The figure section should feel collectible, curated, and systematized.
Not toy-like.
Not scrapbook-like.

## 4. Projects Section
### Layout
Keep the projects section modular so new projects can be added easily later.

### Style treatment
This section should be calmer than the hero and carousel.
It should act as the grounding zone of the site.

Recommended treatment:

- structured grid
- hard-edged project modules
- short labels and concise summaries
- strong visual hierarchy
- fewer ambient glows than the hero
- more reliance on spacing, rules, and type

### Light transition option
If this section lives lower in the page where the design transitions toward a lighter environment, carry over the same sharp-line system into the light mode:

- white or cool gray panels
- black or steel borders
- orange used sparingly for emphasis
- cyan kept minimal

### Desired feeling
This section should read as credible and engineered.
It is where the portfolio proves substance after the cinematic opening.

## Layout-Wide UI Rules
Apply these rules across the whole portfolio:

### Section labeling
Each major section should have a short uppercase label.
Examples of treatment:

- `01 HERO`
- `02 SCENES`
- `03 COLLECTION`
- `04 PROJECTS`

### Dividers
Use strong horizontal rules between sections or within section headers.
These rules should be part of the identity system.

### Containers
Prefer framed containers over floating cards.
Panels should look placed, not drifting.

### Spacing
Use generous spacing, but keep it precise.
The page should breathe without looking soft.

### Copy
Keep descriptions short.
Do not crowd the visuals with too much explanation.

## Non-Goals
To avoid drifting away from the original portfolio:

- do not change the order of the existing sections
- do not remove the hero animation
- do not remove the GIF row
- do not remove the figure carousel
- do not flatten the projects area into a generic template
- do not replace the dark sci-fi palette with a neutral beige luxury palette
- do not make the site feel like a fashion store
- do not over-apply diagonal graphics until the design becomes costume

## Implementation Plan

## Phase 1: Establish The Merged Design System
Create the global visual rules first before touching section-specific polish.

Tasks:

- define CSS variables for the agreed dark, neutral, warm, cool, and light transition colors
- define border, rule-line, and panel tokens
- define typographic tokens for section labels, display headings, body copy, and figure titles
- reduce default roundness across components
- create one reusable framed section wrapper
- create one reusable sharp-edged panel/card style

Goal:
Make the whole site capable of supporting the new style without rewriting layout logic.

## Phase 2: Reskin The Existing Layout Without Changing Structure
Apply the new style to the existing homepage sections in place.

Tasks:

- reskin hero wrapper and hero text treatments
- reskin the 3-GIF row into editorial framed panels
- reskin the figure carousel cards with the new title system and color signature bars
- reskin the projects section into a more structured modular grid
- add consistent section labels and rule lines throughout

Goal:
Preserve the current site architecture while changing the visual language.

## Phase 3: Add Graphic Consistency Across Interactions
Once the static styling is working, tune the interactive states so they feel like part of the same system.

Tasks:

- make hover states sharper and more intentional
- use ember or cyan highlights only at moments of interaction
- convert any soft hover shadows into border, underline, frame, or glow shifts
- ensure carousel motion feels clean inside its new framed system
- add subtle section reveals, line wipes, or panel fades where useful

Goal:
Make the interface feel authored and consistent rather than simply recolored.

## Phase 4: Balance Motion And Readability
Because the site already contains several animated elements, tune the pacing after the reskin is in place.

Tasks:

- ensure the hero remains the primary moving focal point
- make sure the GIF row does not visually overpower the figure carousel
- slow or simplify any motion that competes with readability
- verify that section transitions support the editorial pacing rather than distracting from it
- add reduced-motion fallbacks for autoplay and continuous movement

Goal:
Keep the site cinematic without making it visually exhausting.

## Phase 5: Refine Light-Transition And Portfolio Credibility
Use the lower sections to resolve the page from spectacle into clarity.

Tasks:

- tune the dark-to-light progression between sections
- maintain sharp-line identity even when backgrounds become lighter
- make projects read as serious technical work, not decorative tiles
- confirm that typography, labels, spacing, and rule lines stay consistent in both dark and light zones

Goal:
Create a page that opens with atmosphere and ends with substance.

## Recommended Build Order
To implement the stylistic merge efficiently:

1. global tokens and section wrapper system
2. hero reskin
3. GIF row reskin
4. figure carousel reskin
5. projects section reskin
6. interaction and hover pass
7. responsiveness pass
8. reduced-motion and performance pass

## Final Direction
The portfolio should remain your original site in structure, media, and content rhythm.

The change is that it now presents itself with:

- sharper lines
- stronger framing
- more editorial severity
- more controlled typography
- more disciplined use of color

The result should feel like:

**a cinematic industrial portfolio with luxury-fashion composure**

not:

- a different layout
- a fashion ecommerce clone
- a neon sci-fi overload

If implemented correctly, the site will keep its animation, carousel, GIFs, and project structure exactly as planned, but the surrounding visual language will feel more precise, expensive, and unmistakably designed.
